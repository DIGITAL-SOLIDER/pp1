SOME ORIGAMI ARTS

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT CAMEL

  1. A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back.
  2. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food (milk and meat) and textiles (fiber and felt from hair).

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT PIGEON

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT FYLING CICADA

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT PANDA

  1. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT TEDDY BEAR

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT CHAMELEON

  1. THEIR FEET WORK LIKE SALAD TONGS.
  2. THEY MAINLY CHANGE COLOR IN ORDER TO COMMUNICATE OR REGULATE BODY TEMPERATURE.
